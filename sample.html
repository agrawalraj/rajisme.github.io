de Finetti's Theorem

June 18, 2016 by Raj Agrawal

I personally think everything is easier to understand when put it in terms of money. In this spirit, suppose you were at a casino and had to play game 1, X or game 2, Y. You choose game X with probability p and game Y with probability 1 - p. Further, suppose that the payout of X follows a Pois($\lambda$) and Y follows a Pois($\psi$). We can write our payout as $$\color{red}{\text{Z}_{\text{payout}} = \text{X}\mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}} + \text{Y}(1 - \mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}})}$$, where $\mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}}$ is the indicator random variable that takes 1 with probability p and is independent of X and Y. What is \ $\mathbb{E}(\text{Z} \ | \ \mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}})$? Before answering this, what should $\mathbb{E}(\text{Z} \ | \ \mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}} = 1)$ be? Well this means that you decided to play game 1 which pays out a $Pois(\lambda)$. Hence, this expectation should be $\lambda$. Similarly, $\mathbb{E}(Z \ | \ \mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}} = 1) = \psi$. To answer the first question, we formalize the above a bit by saying that X, Y, and $\mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}}$ are living on the probability space $(\mathbb{P}, \Omega, \mathcal{F})$. Define $A = \{\omega \in \Omega \ \text{s.t.} \ \mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}}(\omega) = 1 \}$ Now from above we have $$\color{red}{(*)} \ \ \ \mathbb{E}(\text{Z} \ | \ \text{A}) = \lambda \text{,} \ \mathbb{E}(Z \ | \ A^c) = \psi$$. Define $$H(\omega) \doteq \mathbb{E}(Z \ | \ \mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}})(\omega) = \lambda \ \text{if} \ \omega \in A \ \ \text{or} \ \psi \ \text{if} \ \omega \in A^c $$ The key thing to recognize here is that our definition of $\mathbb{E}(Z \ | \ \mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}})$ **integrates right**. Shortly, we'll explain what we mean by this. In the above example the sigma-algebra generated by $\mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}}$ is $\mathcal{I} = \{\emptyset, A, A^c, \Omega \}$. We see that for any $E \in \mathcal{I}$, $$\color{red}{(**)} \ \ \ \mathbb{E}(H\mathbb{I}_E) = \mathbb{E}(\mathbb{E}(Z \ | \ \mathbb{I}_{\text{choose game-1}})\mathbb{I}_E)$$.
